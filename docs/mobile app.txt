1. Introduction - What This App Does {#1-introduction}

This mobile application is designed for handymen working with insurance companies (B2A department). Later, we will make it work with other 2 departments, B2B and B2C, for now B2A only. In B2A process It replaces all paper forms, phone calls, and manual processes with a simple step-by-step digital system.
Think of it as a GPS for work of handyman - it shows you exactly what to do next, tracks everything automatically, and makes sure nothing gets missed. You cannot make mistakes because the app only shows you what you need to do right now.

1. What Happens When Handyman Wants to Delete a Visit
Imagine this scenario: John the handyman planned 3 visits to fix a toilet. But after visit 1, he realizes he doesn't need visit 3. What does he do?
In the App: John goes to the job details and sees:
* Visit 1 ? Done
* Visit 2 ? Tomorrow
   * Visit 3 ? Next week [  Delete button] When John clicks Delete:

+-----------------------------+
What Actually Happens:
1. John's message goes to the office
2. Office staff see a new task: "John wants to delete visit 3"
3. Office reviews and approves
4. Visit 3 disappears from John's app
5. Customer gets updated
2. How Dates Work (The Smart Calendar) Think of it like a moving spotlight:
The main calendar in the system always shows the NEXT appointment. It's like a spotlight that moves forward.
Example:
* Monday (Visit 1): System shows "Next appointment: Monday"
* After Monday is done: System automatically updates to "Next appointment: Wednesday" (Visit 2)
* The Monday date is saved in history, but the spotlight moved on
In Simple Terms:
* There's ONE main calendar that always shows what's coming next
* There are 5 "photo albums" that save what happened on each visit
* When visit 1 is done, its date goes into "album 1" and the main calendar updates to show visit 2

UNDERSTANDING THE VISIT SYSTEM - SIMPLE EXPLANATION
What's the Problem We're Solving?
Imagine you're a handyman and you need to fix a broken toilet. Sometimes you can't fix it in one visit:

* Visit 1: You check what's wrong and order parts
* Visit 2: Parts arrived, you install them
* Visit 3: You test everything works
Right now, the system treats each visit like a completely different job. That's confusing!
What's Our Solution?
We create ONE main job (fixing the toilet) and attach multiple visits to it. Like a book with chapters!

Think of it like this:
The Main Job = The whole book
* Customer: John Smith
* Problem: Broken toilet
* Total price: 200 EUR
* Overall progress: 66% done
Each Visit = A chapter in the book
* Chapter 1: Diagnostic (July 15)
* Chapter 2: Installation (July 20)

Protocols are official documents confirming work performed. They serve as:
* Legal proof of work done
* Customer acknowledgment
* Insurance documentation
* Payment basis
7.2 Protocol Types

2. Standard Work Protocol - Normal completed repair
3. Work with Surcharge Protocol - Repair with approved extra payment
4. Surcharge Agreement Protocol - Customer approval document for extra cost
5. Multi-visit Protocol - One protocol per each visit
6. Special Diagnostic Protocol - When price calculated later at home

12.3 AI Components Detail
Material Categorization AI (Miško's Python Bot)
Purpose: Automatically categorize and sum materials
Trigger: Field Popis_a_cena_materialu_z_HM_APP  c populated
Process:
1. Reads text field with all materials
2. Parses each line:
o Material name/description

o Price (with or without VAT)
3. AI categorizes each item:
o Drobný materiál: Screws, tape, small fittings, gaskets
o Náhradné diely: Valves, pipes, major components
4. Sums each category separately
5. Writes totals to respective fields
Output:
* 1_Cena_za_drobny_material_hm c = sum of small items
* 1_Cena_za_nahradne_diely_a_material_hm  c = sum of parts

Purpose: Determine if surcharge needed
